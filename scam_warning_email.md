Thank you for signing up with CoinPort. We are committed to keeping your money safe, which is why we are reaching out with some important information.

We have noticed an increase in sophisticated scams targeting Australians, particularly those living in regional and rural areas. Because you are a new customer, we want to make sure you have the tools to recognize the warning signs before sending money to someone you don't know.

**Common scams to watch out for:**

- **Romance or "Pig Butchering" Scams:** You meet someone online (dating app, social media) who builds a relationship with you. Eventually, they claim to have a "wealthy uncle," a "secret crypto trading bot," or a "mining expert" who can help you make money. They will ask you to sign up for an exchange like CoinPort to do this.
- **Fake Investment Opportunities:** You are approached by a company or person online promising "guaranteed returns" or a "once-in-a-lifetime" investment. They often create fake websites that look real and show massive profits to convince you to deposit more.
- **Recovery Room Scams:** After losing money to a previous scam, you are contacted by someone claiming to be a lawyer, the police, or a "hacker" who can recover your lost funds for an upfront fee. **No one can recover your lost crypto for a fee.**

**Please take a moment to ask yourself:**

1.  **Did someone you have never met in person ask you to sign up for CoinPort?** (This includes friends you've only spoken to online).
2.  **Are you sending cryptocurrency to someone you have never met?** Or to a wallet address they provided?
3.  **Is someone promising you high, guaranteed returns?** If it sounds too good to be true, it is.
4.  **Are you feeling pressured or rushed?** Scammers create urgency so you don't have time to think or ask for advice.

**If you answered "yes" to any of these questions, please hit reply and tell us more about your situation before you send any money.** We are here to help.

If you are simply looking to buy crypto for your own investment, trading, or to send to a verified friend or family member you know in real life, you can disregard this message.
