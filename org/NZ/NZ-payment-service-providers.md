Engaging a New Zealand-based Payment Service Provider (PSP) is an excellent strategy for CoinPort Limited. It can solve critical operational needs: managing high-volume, low-value NZD deposits/withdrawals, offering diverse payment methods, and often providing a more understanding partner than a traditional bank alone. A good PSP acts as a "banking-as-a-service" layer.

Here are New Zealand PSPs to consider, categorized by their potential fit for a crypto exchange.

### **Crucial Selection Criteria for a Crypto Exchange**
When evaluating a PSP, you must assess them on:
*   **Explicit Crypto Policy:** Do they *publicly* or *contractually* accept cryptocurrency businesses? (Many have blanket bans).
*   **API Robustness:** Seamless integration for real-time deposit notifications and payout requests is non-negotiable.
*   **Payment Methods:** Support for POLi, Pay by Bank, debit/credit cards (with strong fraud tools), and potentially online wallets.
*   **Fee Structure:** Understand all costs—setup, monthly, per-transaction, and FX margins.
*   **Compliance Partnership:** Will they work with you on enhanced due diligence, understanding your AML/CFT controls?

### **Recommended NZ Payment Service Providers to Investigate**

#### **Tier 1: Most Promising & Fintech-Focused**
These providers have the mindset and infrastructure most aligned with a tech-driven business like yours.

1.  **Akahu**
    *   **Why they're relevant:** A modern, API-first platform specifically built for **financial data and payments**. They are deeply embedded in the NZ fintech scene and power many innovative financial apps.
    *   **Value Prop:** Provides secure **"Pay by Bank"** (account-to-account) payments via open banking-style connections, which are lower cost and lower fraud risk than cards. Their culture is "developer-first."
    *   **Key Question:** You must inquire directly about their policy on crypto exchanges. Their forward-thinking model makes them a **top-priority candidate**.
    *   **Website:** [akahu.nz](https://akahu.nz)

2.  **Payment Express (a Windcave company)**
    *   **Why they're relevant:** One of NZ's largest and most established PSPs. They offer a full suite: online gateway, POLi, credit/debit cards, and alternative payments.
    *   **Value Prop:** **Scale, stability, and local support.** They have the experience and infrastructure to handle significant transaction volumes. They service a vast range of NZ businesses.
    *   **Key Question:** Their corporate policy on crypto will be decisive. You need to get to their **commercial or enterprise sales team** for a serious discussion, armed with your full compliance pack.
    *   **Website:** [windcave.com](https://www.windcave.com)

3.  **Sylvr (formerly i-Payout)**
    *   **Why they're relevant:** While not exclusively NZ, they have a strong presence and **specialize in high-risk and alternative verticals**, including forex, gaming, and **crypto**. This is a major differentiator.
    *   **Value Prop:** They understand the compliance and risk landscape of sectors traditional banks shy away from. They offer multi-currency accounts, card issuing, and a wide range of payout options.
    *   **Key Question:** They are likely to be receptive. Focus the discussion on your NZ FSPR status and AML programme to align with their risk framework.
    *   **Website:** [sylvr.com](https://www.sylvr.com)

#### **Tier 2: Established Generalists (Require Explicit Policy Check)**
These are major players but may have stricter acceptance policies.

4.  **Worldline (formerly Paymark) & Verifone NZ**
    *   **Why they're relevant:** They process a huge portion of NZ's electronic transactions. They offer omni-channel payment solutions.
    *   **Value Prop:** **Extreme reliability and ubiquity.** If you can get approved, their network is second to none.
    *   **Key Question:** Their risk and compliance teams are very conservative. A direct, professional inquiry via your lawyer or as part of a broader business application is essential. Do not assume they accept crypto.

#### **Tier 3: Strategic Partners for Specific Functions**

5.  **Dosh (by ASB)**
    *   **Why they're relevant:** A digital wallet and payment app from a major bank. Could be explored as a **specific deposit method** for users, similar to POLi.
    *   **Value Prop:** Brand recognition and trust for NZ consumers.
    *   **Key Question:** This would be a supplementary method, not a core banking partner. Requires direct partnership discussion with ASB.

6.  **Dlocal / Airwallex / Stripe**
    *   **Why they're relevant:** These are **global PSPs** with NZD capabilities.
        *   **Airwallex:** Excellent for businesses with international flows; offers multi-currency accounts.
        *   **Stripe:** Very developer-friendly, but has a **history of blocking crypto businesses**. A formal, pre-application inquiry is mandatory.
        *   **Dlocal:** Focuses on emerging markets but handles complex pay-in/pay-out.
    *   **Value Prop:** Global infrastructure, sophisticated APIs, and often better FX rates.
    *   **Key Question:** You must get **written confirmation** of their acceptance policy for a registered NZ FSP crypto exchange before investing in integration.

### **Action Plan for CoinPort Limited**

1.  **Prepare a "PSP Application Pack":** A condensed version of your banking pack. Focus on:
    *   Your NZ company and FSPR number (once obtained).
    *   Summary of your AML/CFT controls (KYC, transaction monitoring, sanctions screening).
    *   Expected transaction volumes and average ticket size.
    *   Clear diagrams of how their PSP integrates into your flow (e.g., user deposits NZD via PSP -> PSP settles to your designated trust account at Heartland/ASB).

2.  **Prioritize and Contact:**
    *   **First:** **Akahu** and **Sylvr**. Their models are the most promising for a tech-forward, compliant crypto business.
    *   **Second:** **Payment Express (Windcave)**. Approach them as a serious enterprise client.
    *   Use your **law firm's commercial contacts** for introductions where possible.

3.  **Ask the Right Questions in Discovery Calls:**
    *   "Do you have an existing policy for serving registered Financial Service Providers operating cryptocurrency exchanges?"
    *   "What enhanced due diligence documents will you require?"
    *   "Can we integrate your API for automated real-time deposit reconciliation and payouts?"
    *   "What are your controls for fraud and chargebacks on card payments?"
    *   "Can you facilitate settlements into a segregated client trust account at our NZ bank?"

### **Realistic Recommendation**

**Start with Akahu and Sylvr in parallel.** Akahu represents the modern, API-driven future of NZ payments and may be the most logical fit. Sylvr’s explicit experience with "high-risk" verticals provides a strong fallback option.

Using a **domestic NZ PSP for NZD customer on/off ramps**, coupled with a **specialist offshore bank (like Bank Frick) for international/corporate treasury**, is a proven and robust operational model for exchanges in jurisdictions like New Zealand.

**Final Note:** Transparency is your greatest asset. By leading with your regulatory status and professional compliance framework, you move the conversation from "*if* we can service you" to "*how* we can best service you."
