### **1.0 EXECUTIVE SUMMARY**

This document outlines the project plan for CoinPort Pty Ltd (ABN 12 624 879 223) to apply for an Australian Financial Services Licence (AFSL) issued by the Australian Securities and Investments Commission (ASIC). The application is driven by the anticipated passage of the *Corporations Amendment (Digital Assets Framework) Bill 2025*, which will require digital asset platform operators to hold an AFSL.

CoinPort is currently registered with AUSTRAC as a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) under DCE registration #100633359. The company is a member of the Australian Financial Complaints Authority (AFCA, Member #100204) and maintains a comprehensive AML/CTF Program (v3.1, effective January 2026). This existing compliance infrastructure provides a strong foundation for the AFSL application.

**Estimated Timeline:** 9–15 months from project commencement to licence grant.

**Estimated Budget:** $120,000–$340,000 (first year, inclusive of all professional fees, insurance, and regulatory costs).

---

### **2.0 PURPOSE**

This project plan serves to:

1. Define the steps required to obtain an AFSL for CoinPort's digital asset exchange operations.
2. Identify responsible parties, timelines, dependencies, and deliverables for each phase.
3. Establish a budget framework for the application process.
4. Ensure CoinPort is licence-ready ahead of any legislative deadline imposed by the Digital Assets Framework Bill.

#### 3.2 Pending Legislation

The *Corporations Amendment (Digital Assets Framework) Bill 2025* was introduced to Parliament in November 2025 and is currently before the Senate Economics Legislation Committee. Key provisions:

- Digital asset platforms will be classified as financial markets or financial product providers under the *Corporations Act 2001*.
- Platform operators will be required to hold an AFSL with appropriate authorisations.

### **4.0 SCOPE OF LICENCE**

#### 4.1 Financial Services to be Authorised

CoinPort must determine the precise financial services authorisations to seek. Based on current operations, the following are anticipated:

| Authorisation | Description | Applicable |
|---|---|---|
| Dealing in a financial product | Buying/selling digital assets on behalf of clients | Yes |
| Making a market in a financial product | Operating an order book / matching engine | Yes |
| Providing custodial or depository services | Holding client digital assets | Yes |
| Providing general financial product advice | Educational content, market commentary | To be assessed |
| Providing personal financial product advice | Tailored investment recommendations | Not anticipated |

#### 4.2 Financial Products

The licence application must specify the classes of financial products. Under the proposed Digital Assets Framework, this is expected to include:

- Digital assets (as defined by the Bill)
- Fiat currency pairs (AUD) associated with digital asset trading

#### 4.3 Recommendation

**Adopt a staged approach.** Apply initially for core authorisations (dealing, market making, custody) and vary the licence later if additional authorisations (e.g., personal advice) become necessary. This reduces complexity and accelerates the initial application.

#### 5.2.1 Responsible Manager Competency Pathways (RG 105)

ASIC requires each RM to demonstrate competency relevant to the financial services being provided. The three primary pathways are:

**Pathway 1 — Qualifications + Experience:**
- Relevant tertiary qualification (e.g., finance, law, accounting, economics)
- Completion of RG 146-compliant training for the relevant financial products
- Minimum 3 years of relevant experience in the last 5 years

**Pathway 2 — Experience Only:**
- Minimum 5 years of relevant industry experience in the last 8 years
- Experience must be directly related to the financial services to be provided

**Pathway 3 — ASIC Assessment:**
- Combination of qualifications and experience that doesn't meet Pathway 1 or 2
- Requires ASIC to assess competency on a case-by-case basis
- Longer processing time — avoid if possible

**Recommendation:** Nicanor Nuqui (current AML/CTF Compliance Officer) is a strong candidate for RM. Assess against Pathway 1 or 2. CoinPort should aim for at least two RMs to provide business continuity and cover the breadth of authorisations sought. The existing AFSL Training Plan (see `policies/afsl-training.md`) provides a structured curriculum.

#### 5.3.1 ASIC General Obligations (s912A) — Checklist

The following obligations must be addressed in the compliance framework:

- [ ] Do all things necessary to ensure financial services are provided efficiently, honestly, and fairly
- [ ] Have adequate arrangements for managing conflicts of interest
- [ ] Comply with licence conditions
- [ ] Comply with financial services laws
- [ ] Take reasonable steps to ensure representatives comply with financial services laws
- [ ] Have adequate risk management systems
- [ ] Have available adequate resources (including financial, technological, and human resources)
- [ ] Maintain organisational competence
- [ ] Ensure representatives are adequately trained and competent
- [ ] Have a dispute resolution system (IDR + EDR)
- [ ] Have adequate compensation arrangements (PI insurance)
- [ ] Comply with Design and Distribution Obligations (DDO)

#### 5.4.1 Financial Requirements (RG 166)

ASIC imposes base-level financial requirements on all AFSL holders. The specific requirements depend on the authorisations held:

| Requirement | Typical Minimum | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Net Tangible Assets (NTA) | $50,000+ | Higher for custodial services; may be $150,000–$500,000+ depending on scale |
| Cash or cash equivalents | 3 months' operating expenses | Must be demonstrable at all times |
| Adjusted Surplus Liquid Funds | Positive at all times | Liquid assets minus current liabilities |
| Annual audit | Required | By a registered company auditor |
| Financial reporting | Annual | Lodged with ASIC within 3 months of financial year-end |

**Note:** The Digital Assets Framework Bill may impose additional or modified financial requirements for digital asset platform operators. Monitor the final legislation closely.

#### 5.4.2 Professional Indemnity Insurance

PI insurance is required under s912B of the Corporations Act as adequate compensation arrangements. Key considerations:

- **Coverage:** Must cover civil liability arising from the provision of financial services (including acts/omissions of representatives)
- **Market:** PI insurance for crypto businesses is a limited and specialised market. Start procurement early.
- **Suggested brokers:** Honan Insurance Group, Marsh, Aon, Gallagher
- **Estimated cost:** $15,000–$50,000 per annum (crypto premiums are significantly elevated)
- **Excess/deductible:** Negotiate carefully — high excesses may not satisfy ASIC

#### 5.6.1 ASIC Processing Times

- **Target processing time:** 150 business days (approximately 7 months)
- **Actual processing time:** Often longer, particularly for novel or complex applications. Cryptocurrency-related applications receive additional scrutiny.
- **RFI rounds:** Expect at least 1–2 rounds of RFIs. Each RFI response typically resets or extends the assessment clock.
- **Tip:** High-quality, complete initial applications with proactive addressing of anticipated ASIC concerns can materially reduce processing time.

#### 5.7.1 Ongoing AFSL Obligations

Once licensed, CoinPort must maintain compliance with the following on an ongoing basis:

- **Annual ASIC levies** — Payable annually based on industry sector and licence class
- **Annual compliance audit** — Independent audit of compliance arrangements
- **Breach reporting** — Report significant breaches to ASIC within 30 calendar days (immediately for gross negligence, fraud, or serious harm)
- **Financial requirements** — Maintain NTA, ASLF, and PI insurance at all times
- **RM notifications** — Notify ASIC within 10 business days of RM appointment or cessation
- **Licence condition changes** — Apply to ASIC for any variation of licence conditions
- **DDO ongoing obligations** — Monitor TMDs, review triggers, report to product issuers
- **Record keeping** — Maintain records for 7 years minimum
