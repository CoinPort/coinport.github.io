Here is a step-by-step guide to the FSP registration process, tailored for your situation as an Australian parent company with a NZ subsidiary.

### **Core Understanding: The Role of the FSPR**
The Financial Service Providers Register (FSPR) is administered by the Companies Office (part of the Ministry of Business, Innovation and Employment - MBIE). Registration is mandatory for anyone "in the business" of providing financial services in New Zealand, which includes operating a cryptocurrency exchange.

---

### **Step-by-Step Registration Process**

#### **Step 1: Determine the Required Financial Service(s)**
You must select the correct financial service category from the Financial Service Providers (Registration and Dispute Resolution) Act 2008. For a cryptocurrency exchange, the most relevant service is typically:

*   **"Operating a Value Transfer Service"** – This is the most common classification for crypto exchanges in NZ. It involves the transfer of value (which includes cryptocurrency) on behalf of the public.
*   You may also need to consider **"Issuing and managing means of payment"** or **"Keeping, investing, administering, or managing money, securities, or investment portfolios on behalf of other persons"** depending on your exact business model (e.g., if you offer custodial wallets or staking services).

**Action:** Review the Financial Markets Authority (FMA)'s guidance on crypto-asset services. The FMA has published statements clarifying that many crypto-asset services are financial services under the law.

#### **Step 2: Appoint New Zealand-Based Governance**
A key requirement for overseas-owned companies is **local direction and management**. The FSPR requires that your New Zealand company has at least:
*   **One director** who lives in New Zealand, **OR**
*   **One director** who lives in Australia and is also a director of an Australian company (this leverages the Trans-Tasman mutual recognition regime, which is perfect for your structure), **AND**
*   **A New Zealand resident** appointed to the role of **"Controller"** for the FSP. The Controller is responsible for liaison with regulators and ensuring compliance. This can be an employee, a director, or a professional service provider.

**Action:** Ensure your CoinPort Limited subsidiary's board composition meets these requirements. You may need to appoint a local NZ resident director or engage a professional corporate trustee/firm to act as your Controller.

#### **Step 3: Join an Approved Dispute Resolution Scheme (DRS)**
This is a **mandatory** and non-negotiable step for most financial services, including value transfer services. You must be a member of an approved scheme *before* you can complete your FSP registration. The two main schemes are:
1.  Financial Services Complaints Ltd (FSCL)
2.  Insurance & Financial Services Ombudsman (IFSO)
3.  Financial Dispute Resolution Service (FDRS)

**Action:** Contact these schemes, provide details of your business, obtain quotes, and sign a membership agreement. You will need your DRS membership number for the FSPR application.

#### **Step 4: Prepare Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Compliance**
While separate from FSP registration, your business must comply with the Anti-Money Laundering and Countering Financing of Terrorism (AML/CFT) Act 2013. Before you start operations, you must:
*   **Register with the New Zealand Police Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU)** as a "Reporting Entity."
*   Have a formal, written **AML/CFT Programme** in place.
*   Appoint an **AML/CFT Compliance Officer**.
*   Conduct a **Risk Assessment**.

**Action:** Develop your AML/CFT programme. It is highly advisable to seek legal advice to ensure it meets NZ standards. Registration with the FIU is done online.

#### **Step 5: Complete the Online FSPR Application**
Once the above prerequisites are satisfied, you can complete the application on the New Zealand Companies Office website.
You will need:
*   Your NZ company details (CoinPort Limited, including its NZBN).
*   Details of all directors and the appointed **Controller**.
*   The chosen **financial service category** (e.g., "Operating a Value Transfer Service").
*   Your **Dispute Resolution Scheme membership number**.
*   Payment for the registration fee (currently approximately NZD $50 for one financial service, plus potential fees for the Companies Office).

**Action:** The director or a person authorised by the director must log in to the Companies Office online services and complete the FSP registration application.

#### **Step 6: Ongoing Compliance and Potential Licensing**
*   **Annual Confirmation:** You must confirm your FSP details annually (similar to your company annual return), pay the fee, and confirm your DRS membership remains current.
*   **Potential for Full Licensing:** Be aware that the FMA is moving towards a full licensing regime for many financial services under the Financial Markets Conduct Act 2013. While "value transfer services" are not currently required to hold an FMA licence, this could change. Monitor FMA communications closely, as you may need to apply for a market service licence in the future.

### **Critical Recommendations for Your Situation**

1.  **Engage Local NZ Professionals:** This process has nuanced legal requirements. **Strongly engage** a New Zealand law firm with expertise in financial services/fintech and a corporate trustee or advisory firm. They can:
    *   Advise on the exact classification of your services.
    *   Act as your resident **Controller**.
    *   Help draft your AML/CFT programme.
    *   Ensure your corporate governance meets regulatory expectations.

2.  **Trans-Tasman Advantage:** Your Australian incorporation can be helpful. Under the Trans-Tasman mutual recognition scheme, an Australian director who is also a director of an Australian company (CoinPort Pty Ltd) can satisfy the requirement for a local director without being NZ resident. However, the separate **Controller** requirement still applies.

3.  **Communicate with the FMA:** For a business of this nature, it can be prudent to engage in pre-application dialogue with the FMA. This helps ensure you are on the right track and understand their expectations regarding your specific business model.

### **Summary Checklist for CoinPort Limited:**
- [ ] Determine precise financial service category (likely "Value Transfer Service").
- [ ] Ensure NZ subsidiary has compliant board/controller structure (NZ resident Controller + AU/NZ director).
- [ ] Join an approved Dispute Resolution Scheme (FSCL, IFSO, or FDRS).
- [ ] Develop a compliant AML/CFT Programme.
- [ ] Register as a Reporting Entity with the NZ Police FIU.
- [ ] Complete the online FSPR application via the NZ Companies Office.
- [ ] Plan for ongoing annual confirmations and monitor for future FMA licensing requirements.

By following these steps and leveraging professional advice, you can establish your New Zealand subsidiary as a compliant and registered Financial Service Provider.
