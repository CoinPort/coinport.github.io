# Important security check for your CoinPort account

Hi {{FirstName}},

Thank you for signing up with CoinPort. We are committed to keeping your money safe, so before you send your first payment we want to walk you through some warning signs of common scams.

We've noticed an increase in sophisticated scams targeting Australians, particularly older Australians and those living in regional and rural areas. Because you're a new customer, we want to make sure you can recognise the warning signs before sending money to someone you don't know.

## Common scams to watch out for

1. **Romance or "pig butchering" scams** — You meet someone online (dating app, social media, messaging app) who builds a relationship with you, sometimes over weeks or months. Eventually they introduce a "wealthy uncle," a "secret crypto trading bot," or a "mining/trading expert" who can help you make money, and ask you to sign up for an exchange like CoinPort to do this.
2. **Fake investment opportunities** — A company or person online promises "guaranteed returns" or a "once-in-a-lifetime" investment. They often show a fake trading dashboard or website with a rising balance to convince you to deposit more.
3. **Recovery room scams** — After losing money to a previous scam, you're contacted by someone claiming to be a lawyer, police officer, government official, or "ethical hacker" who can recover your lost funds — for an upfront fee. **No one can recover lost crypto for a fee.**
4. **Impersonation scams** — Someone contacts you claiming to be from your bank, the ATO, myGov, the police, or even CoinPort, saying your account is compromised or you owe money, and instructs you to move funds into crypto to "protect" or "verify" it.
5. **Job and task scams** — You're offered easy paid work (liking videos, "optimising" listings, data entry) that requires you to first deposit crypto, or to receive and forward crypto or money on someone else's behalf. Moving funds for someone else can make you a money mule, even if you didn't know that was the plan.
6. **Remote access scams** — Someone asks you to install remote access software (e.g. AnyDesk, TeamViewer, AirDroid) or share your screen so they can "help" you invest, fix a problem, or process a refund.

## Before you send any money, ask yourself

1. Did someone you have never met in person ask you to sign up for CoinPort? (This includes people you've only spoken to online or by phone.)
2. Are you sending cryptocurrency to someone you've never met in person, or to a wallet address someone else gave you?
3. Is someone promising high, guaranteed, or risk-free returns? If it sounds too good to be true, it is.
4. Are you feeling pressured or rushed, or have you been told to keep this transaction secret from family, friends, or your bank?
5. Has anyone asked you to receive or forward crypto on their behalf, or to open an account and share the login details with them?
6. Have you been told what to say — or told to lie about the purpose of this transaction — if we, your bank, or anyone else asks?
7. Are you being asked to pay a fee, "tax," or "unlock cost" before you can access funds, a prize, or a previous investment?
8. Did this opportunity start with an unexpected call, text, email, or social media message or ad?
9. Has someone asked you to install remote access or screen-sharing software, or to stay on a call with them while you complete this transaction?

If you answered "yes" to any of these questions, please **stop and reply to this email** to tell us more about your situation before sending any money. We're here to help, and there's no such thing as a silly question — we would always rather ask than let you lose money.

If you're simply buying crypto for your own investment or trading, or to send to a verified friend or family member you know in real life, you can disregard this message.
