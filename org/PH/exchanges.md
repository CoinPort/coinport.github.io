### ⚖️ Local vs. International Exchanges

When choosing an exchange, the main trade-off is between regulation and features.

**Local Platforms (Coins.ph & PDAX)**
These platforms are regulated by the **Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas (BSP)**.
*   **Pros:** You can sell crypto and withdraw PHP to your bank or GCash instantly without finding a buyer. They are legally compliant and generally safer for newcomers.
*   **Cons:** They usually offer a smaller selection of cryptocurrencies and charge slightly higher trading fees (around **0.6%**).

**International Platforms (Bybit & Binance)**
These platforms use a **Peer-to-Peer (P2P)** system for PHP withdrawals.
*   **Pros:** You get access to hundreds of coins and very low trading fees (as low as **0.1%**).
*   **Cons:** You must manually match with a seller. While the platform holds the crypto in escrow for safety, you rely on the seller to send you the PHP via bank transfer.

### 💡 Quick Tips for Withdrawing
*   **Use GCash/Maya/InstaPay:** For the fastest and cheapest transfers, look for sellers or platforms that support **InstaPay** (fees ~₱10-₱15) or direct e-wallet transfers.
*   **Watch the Fees:** While P2P trades are "free," sellers often build a small premium into the exchange rate. Local platforms have explicit fees (e.g., 0.6% trade + ₱10 withdrawal).
*   **Complete KYC Verification:** You must verify your identity to withdraw PHP. Higher verification levels unlock higher transaction limits (e.g., Coins.ph allows up to ₱10 Million daily for fully verified users).
